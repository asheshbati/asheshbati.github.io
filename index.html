I'm Ashesh Bati, currently a graduate student at the London School of Geometry and Number Theory (LSGNT). I am interested in algebraic geometry, and currently I'm working on derived categories and Bridgeland stability. I'm also interested in neighbouring areas such as enumerative geometry, and anything to do with sheaves.

Maths

I'm interested in derived categories and stability. Related to this, I like to work with sheaves and generally with their moduli. These are all part of the wider area of Algebraic Geometry.

Currently, I am looking into the derived categories of nodal varieties (with Richard Thomas) and Bridgeland Stability and wall-crossing (with Soheyla Feyzbakhsh). To get a feel for these, these resources are quite good:
Derived categories
Bridgeland Stability

Previously, during my masters' I primarily looked at enumerative invariants, in particular the GW/DT correspondence and its proof in the toric case.
